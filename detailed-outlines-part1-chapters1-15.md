## Chapter 1: Paint and Perspective

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Sienna Lawson, first person  
**Setting:** Visual Arts Studio, Blackwood Arts Academy  
**Time:** Early September, late afternoon

**Purpose:** Introduce Sienna's character, artistic style, and boarding school world before the collaboration project announcement.

**Beats:**
- Sienna works on abstract painting in her studio corner—bold reds and oranges, music blasting, completely absorbed in spontaneous creative process; describes her approach to art through internal monologue
- Madison Clarke interrupts to drag her to dinner; through their banter establish Sienna is scholarship student, senior year, passionate about emotional expression; Madison is theatrical roommate and best friend
- Dining hall scene establishes Blackwood social hierarchies; Sienna observes music students eating together—disciplined, serious, slightly intimidating; casual mention of upcoming interdisciplinary project that pairs different art forms
- Evening assembly in Willoughby Hall where Professor Reeves announces collaboration project details; partnerships assigned not chosen to push students outside comfort zones; twenty percent of semester grade; Sienna hopes for visual artist or dancer partner

**Dialogue Topics:** Madison teasing about losing track of time and paint in hair; discussion of what makes good art—emotion versus technique; speculation about potential collaboration partners; Professor Reeves's formal announcement of project parameters and expectations

**Emotional Arc:** Sienna feels confident and in control in her creative space, comfortable with her spontaneous style. Slight anxiety about project but optimistic about getting compatible partner.

**Key Details:** Paint-splattered floors, north-facing studio windows, canvases stacked everywhere; Willoughby Hall's grand formal space; communal dining with long tables; timeless boarding school aesthetic—brick buildings, preppy dress code

**Emotional Arc:** Sienna moves from tiredness to surprise at Nathan's vulnerability to relief at sharing her own fears. Deep emotional connection forms—they understand each other's hidden struggles.
